    • Dynamo

    In Stock

    Technical Components

  • Armature: The rotating coil of wire where the electrical current is induced.
  • Field Magnet (Stator): Produces a constant magnetic field. Small dynamos use permanent magnets, while larger ones use electromagnets.
  • Commutator: A rotary switch that converts the generated alternating current (AC) into pulsing direct current (DC).
  • Brushes: Graphite blocks that maintain contact with the commutator to extract current.

    • Operating Principles & Data

  • Generator Principle: Based on Faraday's Law of Electromagnetic Induction—voltage is induced by rotating a conductor within a magnetic field.
  • Output Type: Direct Current (DC).
  • Voltage/Current Characteristics: Dependent on rotational speed, strength of the magnetic field, and the number of turns in the coil.
  • Efficiency: High-end, small-scale dynamos (e.g., Velogical rim dynamo) can achieve over 60% efficiency at 30 kph.
  • Lifespan: A well-maintained dynamo can last 10–15 years.

    • Example Specifications (Bicycle Dynamo)

  • Voltage: 6V
  • Power Output: 1.5W to 3W (standard for bicycle lighting)
  • Weight: 60g – 90g (miniature rim type)
  • Activation Speed: Typically, full output is achieved at speeds above 15 kph (9.3 mph).

    • Types of Dynamos

  • Self-Excited: Uses the current it produces to generate its own magnetic field.
  • Separately Excited: Requires an external power source to create its magnetic field.
  • Series/Shunt/Compound: Different configurations of armature and field windings, often found in industrial, older applications.
